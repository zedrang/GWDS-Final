CARP

These are my three poster redesigns for the CARP (Contrast, Alignment, Repetition, and Proximity) task.

In my first design I stayed very simple and barely
changed any of the elements that I was given.
I aligned all the logos into the bottom right
corner with a repeating size and shape for all of
them. I decided to highlight the main text to give
it some contrast with the background, but the
rest of the page seemed jumbled and looks like
a mess overall with not much thought to
proximity, so I decided to make drastic changes
with my second design.


For my second design I used a much simpler
background which allows for better contrast with
my now all white text. This combined with the
repeating font across all text allows for the
reader to be able to understand the poster more
and take in the information better. I've removed
the glaring backgrounds from the logos and
aligned them all to the bottom of the page where
they're not covering the background. I also
incorporated a balloon into the special O of the
logo and positioned the main body of text
around the curve of the big balloon.

My final design for the poster built upon my second
design but enhanced it with the use of some more Radial like
text around the big balloon and by moving the background up
revealing a brighter sky that makes the whole poster pop
more. I've moved the date down the bottom onto the colourful
balloon background where it can catch your eye more and by
moving it all onto one line people are less likely to
confuse the dates for times. I've also smoothed out the
logos more, so they're more closely aligned with each other
in size and uniformity on the colourful background and made
more separate from the main section of the poster. Finally,
I've again slightly offset each line of the main body of
text so it curves around the balloon, but as well this time
Ive exaggerated certain words with font size, boldness, and
underlining to draw in the eye show off the exciting
prospects of the event.
